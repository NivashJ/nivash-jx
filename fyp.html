The intricacies of Tamil maintenance and shift in Singapore

Final Year Project | Undergraduate Thesis
Linguistics & Multilingual Studies
Nanyang Technological University

As part of the requirements to fulfill my undergraduate degree, I was expected to undertake a research project that best displayed my interest in the field of linguistics. Over the course of two semesters, I completed a socioinguistics dissertation on the language situation of the Tamils in Singapore.

Having an interest in the field of Language Maintenance and Language Shift (LMLS) and minortiy languages, I used this opportunity to investigate the realities of how the linguistic phenomena of language shift has affected the Tamils of Singapore.

While all three main ethnic groups have experienced language shift, the Tamils and the wider Indian community have been at the forefront of conversations surrounding the shift to English. The Indians of Singapore were the first of the three main ethnic groups to shift to English. Consequentially, Tamil was the only official language to have been overtaken by English in 2010.

The concerning downward trend of reported Tamil use at home has sparked questions on the future of the language in Singapore; many have predicted that Tamil will soon be moribund in Singapore, at least as a spoken langauge. The paper investigates the language use, attitudes and core values of Tamil Singaporeans to paint a a more nuanced picture of Tamils' language siutation which the census is unable to capture.
